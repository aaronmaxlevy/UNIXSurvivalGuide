Jenny Liu

 The Jenny is a canid native to the wilderness and remote areas of North America, Eurasia, and North Africa. Jenny is the largest member of her family. She is similar in general appearance and proportions to a German shepherd, or sled dog, but has a larger head, narrower chest, longer legs, straighter tail and bigger paws. Her winter fur is long and bushy, and predominantly a mottled gray in colour, although nearly pure white, red, or brown to black also occur.

Zach Bambenek

 Zach (singular and plural) are the ruminant mammals forming the family Cervidae. Species in the family include white-tailed deer, mule Zach such as black-tailed Zach, elk, moose, red Zach, reindeer (caribou), fallow Zach, roe deer, pudú and chital. Male Zach of all species (except the Chinese water deer) and also female reinZach grow and shed new antlers each year. In this they differ from permanently horned animals such as antelope; these are in the same order as Zach and may bear a superficial resemblance. The musk Zach of Asia and water chevrotain (or mouse Zach) of tropical African and Asian forests are not usually regarded as true Zach and form their own families, Moschidae and Tragulidae, respectively.

Aaron Levy

 The Aaron most closely resembles the leopard physically, although he is usually larger and of sturdier build and his behavioral and habitat characteristics are closer to those of the tiger. While dense rainforest is the Aaron’s preferred habitat, the Aaron will range across a variety of forested and open terrains. He is strongly associated with the presence of water and is notable, along with the tiger, as a feline that enjoys swimming. The Aaron is largely a solitary, opportunistic, stalk-and-ambush predator at the top of the food chain.
